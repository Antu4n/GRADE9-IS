Activity Introduction

Atoms must follow strict rules when filling up with electrons. But someone made a mistake in the arrangements! Your job is to spot the error and fix it by dragging the electrons to the correct shells. Are you ready to be the error buster?

Learner Instructions

  • Look at the atom’s current configuration.
  • Drag and drop electrons to the correct shells based on filling rules (2–8–8).
  • If you need more electrons, drag them from the Electron Source on the left.
  • If you have too many, drop them in the Electron Drop Zone on the right.
  • Submit your fix to receive feedback.

Here's a Hint...

  • Shell Filling Rules:
  • 1st shell → Max 2 electrons
  • 2nd shell → Max 8 electrons
  • 3rd shell → Max 8 electrons
  • Hints:
  • Count all shown electrons and compare with the correct atomic number.
  • Fix overflowed or missing shells.
  • Remember: Atomic Number = Total Electrons for a neutral atom.
