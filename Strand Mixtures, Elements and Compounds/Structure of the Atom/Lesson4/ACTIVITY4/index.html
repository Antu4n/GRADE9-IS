💡 Key Reminders:

  • The valence shell is the outermost electron shell.
  • Atoms with 1–3 valence electrons usually lose them during bonding.
  • Atoms with 5–7 valence electrons usually gain electrons to fill their shell.
  • A full outer shell means the atom is stable and not reactive.
