Activity Introduction

Every atom has a unique structure. In this activity, you will use dots or crosses to sketch electrons in the correct energy levels. Think like a chemist and draw like a scientist!

Learner Instructions

  • Click on each shell (the dashed rings) to place electrons as crosses (×).
  • Use the atom’s atomic number to determine the total number of electrons.
  • Follow the filling rules:
    • 1st shell: maximum 2 electrons
    • 2nd shell: maximum 8 electrons
    • 3rd shell: maximum 8 electrons
  • Use the tools provided:
    • Undo: Remove the last electron you placed.
    • Clear: Start over and clear all electrons.
    • Check: Submit your diagram for feedback.

Here's a Hint...

  • Electron Shell Rules:
  • Shell 1 → Up to 2 electrons
  • Shell 2 → Up to 8 electrons
  • Shell 3 → Up to 8 electrons
  • Examples:
  • Hydrogen (1) → 1 dot in 1st shell
  • Carbon (6) → 2 in 1st, 4 in 2nd → 2.4
  • Sodium (11) → 2 in 1st, 8 in 2nd, 1 in 3rd → 2.8.1
